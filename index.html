By Siddharth Chikalkar


The theoretical model that elucidates the student life cycle affected factory leading to student dropouts is multifaceted. It encompasses various factors including academic challenges, personal circumstances, financial constraints, and institutional issues that influence a student's educational journey. Data sources pertinent to this model encompass student demographics, academic performance records, socio-economic backgrounds, and feedback mechanisms. These are supplemented by methods such as statistical analysis, machine learning algorithms, and qualitative assessments to comprehend patterns and anticipate potential dropout risks. Leveraging this data-driven model enables institutions to identify at-risk students, provide timely interventions, and tailor support mechanisms crucial to mitigating dropout rates. The analysis generated aids in developing proactive strategies and personalized approaches to enhance student retention and success within educational settings.
